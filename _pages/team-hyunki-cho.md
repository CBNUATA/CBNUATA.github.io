<p>Senior Researcher</p>
<p>Korea Testing &amp; Research Institute (KTR)</p>
<p>Medical Device Evaluation Center, Gwacheon, Republic of Korea</p>

<p>I hold a Dr. rer. nat. in Pharmacy (Pharmazie) from Universität des Saarlandes, Germany, completed under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Markus R. Meyer and Prof. Dr. Alexandra K. Kiemer, with doctoral research conducted at the Environmental Safety Group, KIST-Europe (Saarbrücken). My doctoral thesis developed an Adverse Outcome Pathway (AOP) for 5α-reductase inhibition and its environmental implications for endocrine disruption. I received my M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Industrial Crop Science and Technology (with a double major in Biochemistry) from Chungbuk National University, Korea.</p>

<p>My research has spanned <strong>endocrine disruptor toxicity assessment</strong> — building AOPs across in vitro models (H295R, 293FT, SF9, zebrafish embryos, <em>Daphnia magna</em>), non-targeted metabolomics/lipidomics via QTOF HR-MS, CYP450 metabolism, and hormone receptor reporter assays — as well as <strong>environmental and tobacco-smoke toxicology</strong> and, currently, <strong>chemical characterization for medical device biocompatibility</strong>. I'm interested in bridging these areas into an integrated chemical safety-assessment framework that connects analytical chemistry with mechanistic toxicology.</p>
