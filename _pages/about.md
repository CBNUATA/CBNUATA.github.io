I am a Senior Researcher on the Chemical Characterization Team at KTR's Medical Device Evaluation Center, where I work on biocompatibility and extractables & leachables (E&L) analysis for medical devices, primarily within the ISO 10993-18 framework.

I hold a Dr. rer. nat. in Pharmacy from Saarland University, Germany, completed under the supervision of Prof. Markus R. Meyer and Prof. Alexandra K. Kiemer, with doctoral research conducted at KIST-Europe. I received my B.S. and M.S. in Industrial Plant Science (double major in Biochemistry) from Chungbuk National University.

My research sits at the intersection of two areas: **in vitro toxicology** — thyroid receptor reporter assays, CYP450 characterization, multi-omics approaches (lipidomics, metabolomics, transcriptomics), *Daphnia magna* and zebrafish ecotoxicology, and the Adverse Outcome Pathway (AOP) framework — and **chemical characterization**, including non-targeted analysis by GC/LC-Orbitrap mass spectrometry and semi-quantification under KOLAS/ISO 17025 accreditation. My long-term goal is to bridge these two pillars into an integrated chemical safety-assessment framework.
